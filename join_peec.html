How do I join the team?

Doing research can be a tedious and challenging process, but it can also be a rewarding and fulfilling experience. If you would like to join the PEEC lab, please follow the steps below:

To Ph.D. Applicants

Requirements

We welcome Ph.D. applicants who are interested in power electronics, renewable energy generation, transportation electrification, advanced energy storage systems, and related fields, as well as industry professionals:

  1. Bachelor's or Master's degree in engineering, including but not limited to Electrical Engineering, Computer Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, Energy Science Engineering, etc.
  2. Able to, or willing to learn to, use English communication and writing for academics.

What do we do?

Applicants meeting any one or more of the following criteria will be prioritized:

  1. Experience in the design of power electronics circuits, magnetics design, and power-circuits testing.
  2. Experience with embedded control (such as DSP, FPGA) of power electronics, hardware-in-the-loop (HIL) simulation, programming, and debugging.
  3. Strong foundation in applied mathematics, with a background in control or optimization, and an interest in applying algorithms to electrical systems.

How to Apply

Prospective students should send an email with the subject line "Prospective Ph.D. <Your Name> " to the email address cuixf@stanford.edu for inquiries. The email should be written in English and include the following:

  1. A CV/Resume in English
  2. Unofficial transcripts
  3. Other materials demonstrating research and work capabilities (e.g., published papers as the first author, papers in preparation, project reports, etc.)

Strong applicants will be contacted for a phone interview. All applicants must meet UCLA's graduate admission requirements.

Graduate Students

We are actively seeking motivated graduate students from diverse backgrounds, such as electrical engineering, power and energy engineering, applied mathematics, computer engineering, mechanical engineering, and more to join our research group. We are committed to fostering a supportive and inclusive environment for all our students and believe that diversity enriches our research endeavors.

If you are interested in pursuing graduate studies and would like to join our team, we encourage you to apply to the ECE Department’s graduate program. Be sure to specify Xiaofan Cui as a potential mentor in your personal statement.

Prospective students are also welcome to contact Dr. Cui directly to discuss opportunities. Please send your CV and a brief description of your research interests in an email to Dr. Cui, with a subject line as “Prospective M.S. <Your Name>” or “Prospective Ph.D. <Your Name>”.

Undergraduate Students

We are always delighted to welcome undergraduate students who are interested in conducting research projects with us. We believe that working with students can bring a fresh perspective and new ideas to our team, and we are committed to providing an inclusive and supportive environment for all our researchers. Ideally, we are looking for students who can commit to working with us for at least one semester. This will allow you to gain valuable experience, get involved in ongoing projects, and make a meaningful contribution to our research goals.

If you are interested, please email your CV and a brief description of your research interests to Dr. Cui, with a subject line as “Undergrad <Your Name>”.

Visiting Students/Scholars

We welcome visiting students/scholars who are interested in collaborating with us. Ideally, students/scholars will be able to visit for a minimum of six months (preferably one year) to maximize the opportunity for collaboration and research. We can accommodate in-person/virtual visits but the funding is not guaranteed and self-funded visiting is preferred. If you are interested in exploring the possibility of working with us, please email Dr. Cui with a subject line as “Visiting <Your Name>”.

Postdoctoral Researchers

The availability of postdoctoral positions varies depending on the funding conditions. If you are interested in our research focus, you are encouraged to contact Dr. Cui to explore potential opportunities. To apply, please include your CV, a brief summary of your research experience and interests, and the contact information for at least three references, with a subject line as “Postdoc <Your Name>”.
